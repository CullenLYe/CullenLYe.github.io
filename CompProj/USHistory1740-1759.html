1740-1759

Presidents During This Period:
N/A

Notable Events:
1740: The University of Pennsylvania is founded by George Whitefield, an evangelist, as a place of worship.
1741: Alaska is discovered by Danish explorer, Vitus Jonassen Bering.
1743: The American Philosophical Society is founded in Philadelphia, a place where people could share ideas. Some notable visitors were: George Washington, Benjamin Franklin, etc.
1750: Great Britain passes the Iron Act, restricting the American iron industry.
1751: Great Britain passes the Currency Act, restricting the distribution of paper money and the establishment of new banks by the American colonies.
1754: French and Indian War begins, pitting the American colonies against France, with both sides supported by Native American tribes.
1756: England officially declares war on France, backing up the American colonies.
