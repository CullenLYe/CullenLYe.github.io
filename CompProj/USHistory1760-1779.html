1760-1779

Presidents During This Period:
N/A

Notable Events:
1760: King George III becomes the king of Britain.
1763: The French and Indian War ends. England is given all of the French territories east of the Mississippi River. The Spanish also gives up land.
1763: The British give the Proclamation of 1763, prohibiting settlers from expanding westward. This began fueling the colonists' rage.
1764: Britain passes Sugar Act to help pay for war debts. It increased taxes on molasses, textiles, coffee, etc. It further increased taxes on foreign goods.
1764: The Currency Act is passed, prohibiting the colonists from issuing paper currency. This threatened to hurt the colonial economy, angering colonists.
1764: James Otis protests the recent acts passed by Britain, coining the famous catchphrase, "Taxation without representation". People begin boycotting British goods.
1765: Stamp Act taxed the colonists on all printed materials. The tax money went directly to Britain, instead of the colonies. The Sons of Liberty are formed, a secret organization opposed to the Stamp Act.
1765: Quartering Act required colonists to provide shelter for British troops and to supply them. British General Thomas Gage is sent to enforce it.
1766: After a lot of rioting and civil disobedience, King George III repeals the Stamp Act. In its place, the Declaratory Act is passed, giving the British colonies unlimited power to govern the American colonies.
1767: The Townshend Revenue Acts, a new series of taxes on the American colonies, are passed. Boycotts continue.
1768: Samuel Adams writes the Massachusetts Circular Letter, protesting the Townshend Acts.
1770: The Boston Massacre takes place, in which British soldiers open fire upon American colonists, fueling the colonists' rage.
1770: The Townshend Acts and Quartering Act are repealed.
1773: Tea Act is passed, taxing tea imports.
1773: The Boston Tea Party takes place, in which a group of supposed Sons of Liberty sneak onto British ships carrying tea. They proceed to spill nearly one million dollars, in today's worth, of tea into the harbor.
1774: Britain begins passing the Coercive Acts (Intolerable Acts), a series of laws in protest to the Boston Tea Party. Commerical shipping to Boston was shut down until the colonists paid their taxes.
1774: Britain passes the Massachusetts Regulating Act and the Massachusetts Government Act, effectively ending colonial self-rule.
1774: Britain brings the Quartering Act back. They also begin confiscating the colonists' weapons.
1774: The First Continental Congress meets, with representatives from all the colonies except Georgia. They uphold the colonists' individual rights and reprimands the British for passing oppressive laws.
1775: The colonists begin preparing for a potential war. Patrick Henry gives his famous speech, saying "Give me liberty or give me death!"
1775: British General Gage is ordered to destroy the colonists' weapons at Lexington and Concord. Paul Revere travels all the way there from Boston at midnight to warn the people there.
1775: British troops confronted militiamen at Lexington when someone fired a shot. That shot is called "the shot heard around the world" because it led to an all out fight. This started the American Revolution. As the British retreated, many more were killed and wounded by stray farmers and rebels.
1775: American soldiers are mobilized, and volunteers begin to take Boston back from the British.
1775: The Second Continental Congress appoints George Washington to lead the new Continental Army.
1775: At the Battle of Bunker Hill, the Americans kill over a thousand British troops while only losing 400.
1775: The Americans offer peace via the Olive Branch Petition, but King George III refuses.
1775: The American Navy is created.
1776: Thomas Paine's pamphlet, "Common Sense, is spread around the colonies, rallying many colonialists against Britain.
1776: France and Spain begin supporting the Americans.
1776: The Declaration of Independence is created by Thomas Jefferson, Bemjamin Franklin, John Adams, etc. It is signed and publicized.
1776: George Washington continues suffers defeat after defeat.
1776: Nathan Hale, an American spy, is caught and executed without a trial, his last words being, "I only regret that I have but one life to lose for my country."
1776: On Christmas, Washington takes his troops across the Delaware River into Pennsylvania, surprising and defeating the British there. This harrowing endeavour greatly boosts American morale.
1777: America adopts its original flag with 13 stars.
1777: John Paul Jones, the "father of the American Navy" goes berserk and attacks many British coastal cities while avoiding British capture.
1777: The Battle of Saratoga, the first major American victory, takes place.
1777: Congress adopts the Articles of Confederation.
1778: The French officially ally with America with the signing of the Treaty of Amity and Commerce and Treaty of Alliance.
1778: The British rally Native Americans against the British. Iroquois Indians begin to attack and massacre colonists.
1778: Benjamin Franklin is appointed as the American representative for France.
1779: Spain declares war on Britain.
1779: John Adams is appointed to negotiate peace with England.
