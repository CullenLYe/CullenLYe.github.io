1800-1819

Presidents During This Period:
1796-1800: John Adams
1800-1808: Thomas Jefferson
1808-1816: James Madison
1816-1824: James Monroe


Notable Events:
1800: John Chapman, better known as Johnny Appleseed (yes, he was a real person), begins kindly handing out apple seedlings to Ohio settlers.
1800: The U.S. Library of Congress, the research library that officially serves Congress, is founded.
1803: The United States buys the Louisiana territory from France for $15 million, which was called the Louisiana Purchase.
1804: Lewis and Clark begin their journey to map out the northwest United States.
1804: Alexander Hamilton dies in the infamous Burr-Hamilton duel, which was the culmination of the rivalry between Aaron Burr and Hamilton.
1807: Thomas Jefferson signs the Act Prohibiting Importation of Slaves of 1807, attempting to ban the transatlantic slave trade.
1812: The War of 1812 takes place, in which the United States successfully beat the British again.
1817: Construction of the Erie Canal begins.
1818: The United States Congress officially adopts the flag with 13 stars.
