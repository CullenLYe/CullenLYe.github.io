1820-1839

Presidents During This Period:
1816-1824: James Monroe
1824-1828: John Quincy Adams
1828-1836: Andrew Jackson
1836-1840: Martin Van Buren


Notable Events:
1820: The whaling ship Essex sinks after being attacked by a sperm whale, inspiring Moby Dick, a classic American novel.
1821: The Adams-Onis Treaty is ratified, in which Spain gives Florida to the United States.
1823: James Monroe, the current U.S. President, issues the Monroe Doctrine, a policy opposing European colonialism in the Americas.
1823: Joseph Smith claims to have a vision of an angel, and he creates the religion Mormonism. 1826: Thomas Jefferson and John Adams die on the 50th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence.
1827: The Baltimore and Ohio Railroad is created, the first United States railroad for the transportation of people and goods.
1831: The first American bank robbery occurs in New York.
1831: Indians start getting forcibly relocated from their ancestral homelands, beginning the Trail of Tears.
1831: Nat Turner, an enslaved preacher, leads a violent rebellion against the whites, killing many of them.
1837: The Panic of 1837 occurs, in which the economy quickly suffered, increasing unemployment.
