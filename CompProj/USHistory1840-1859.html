1840-1859

Presidents During This Period:
1836-1840: Martin Van Buren
1840-1841: William Henry Harrison
1841-1844: John Tyler
1844-1848: James K. Polk
1848-1850: Zachary Taylor
1850-1852: Millard Fillmore
1852-1856: Franklin Pierce
1856-1860: James Buchanan


Notable Events:
1840: Charles Wilkes explores Antarctica, claiming it for America.
1842: The Webster-Ashburton Treaty officially establishes the boundary between the United States and Canada.
1844: Samuel B. Morse sends the first telegraph message, "What hath God wrought?", forever changing technology.
1845: Irish immigrants begin pouring into America as a result of the Irish Potato Famine.
1846: The Mexican-American War begins.
1847: The American Medical Association is founded.
1847: Frederick Douglas, a black abolitionist, creates The North Star, an anti-slavery newspaper.
1848: Gold is discovered in California, starting the California Gold Rush.
1848: The Mexican-American War ends with the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo, granting America a lot of land.
1849: Elizabeth Blackwell becomes the first woman to obtain a medical degree.
1849: Harriet Tubman begins her countless missions to help slaves escape north via the Underground Railroad.
1850: The Fugitive Slave Act is passed, providing for the return of escaped slaves.
1852: Harriet Beecher Stowe publishes Uncle Tom's Cabin an anti-slavery American classic.
1854: The Gadsden Purchase takes place, in which the United States bought Mexican territory that would later become Arizona and New Mexico.
1854: Bleeding Kansas, a series of violent confrontations over the legality of slavery in Kansas, begins.
1854: The Republican Party is founded by anti-slavery Whigs.
1857: The Dred Scott v. Sandford Supreme Court case essentially ruled that the Constitution was not meant to include American citizenship for black people.
