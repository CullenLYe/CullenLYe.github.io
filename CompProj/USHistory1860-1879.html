1860-1879

Presidents During This Period:
1856-1860: James Buchanan
1860-1865: Abraham Lincoln
1865-1868: Andrew Johnson
1868-1876: Ulysses S. Grant
1876-1880: Rutherford B. Hayes


Notable Events:
1861: Jefferson Davis becomes the first and only President of the Confederate States.
1861: The Battle of Fort Sumter happens, in which the Confederate States beat the Union in battle, starting the American Civil War.
1861: The American Civil War begins, dividing the United States into the Union and the Confederacy.
1861: Bleeding Kansas ends.
1862: President Abraham Lincoln encourages Western migration by passing the Homestead Act, granting settlers land as a reward.
1863: Lincoln gives his Gettysburg Address, truly inspiring the Union to keep fighting for what's right.
1865: President Lincoln is assassinated by John Wilkes Booth.
1865: Confederate General Robert E. Lee surrenders to Union General Ulysses S. Grant, effectively ending the war.
1867: Russia sells Alaska to the United States.
1869: A golden spike is driven into the first American transcontinental railroad upon its completion.
1870: John D. Reockefeller creates the Standard Oil Company.
1871: The Great Chicago Fire begins, engulfing much of Chicago in flames.
1872: President Ulysses S. Grant makes Yellowstone the first national park.
1873: The James-Younger Gang commits the first successful train robbery.
1876: Mark Twain publishes the American classic, The Adventures of Tom Sawyer.
1876: The Battle of the Little Bighorn, or Custer's Last Stand, occurs, in which the United States is defeated by many Native American tribes.
1879: Thomas Edison presents his first light bulb.
