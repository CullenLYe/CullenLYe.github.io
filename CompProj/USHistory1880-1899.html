1880-1899

Presidents During This Period:
1876-1880: Rutherford B. Hayes
1880-1881: James A. Garfield
1881-1884: Chester A. Arthur
1884-1888: Grover Cleveland
1888-1892: Benjamin Harrison
1892-1896: Grover Cleveland again
1896-1901: William McKinley


Notable Events:
1882: Thomas Edison begins supplying electricity to homes.
1885: The United States receives the Statue of Liberty from France.
1888: The Great Blizzard of 1888 strikes, turning out to be one of the worst blizzards in U.S. history.
1890: Jacob Riis publishes How the Other Half Lives, beginning working condition reforms.
1890: The Sherman Antitrust Act is passed, outlawing monopolies.
1898: The USS Maine explodes, prompting the start of the Spanish-American War, which ends in the same year.
1899: The Philippine-American War breaks out, in which the Philippines fight for their independence.
