1920-1939

Presidents During This Period:
1912-1920: Woodrow Wilson
1920-1923: Warren G. Harding
1923-1928: Calvin Coolidge
1928-1932: Herbert Hoover
1932-1945: Franklin D. Roosevelt


Notable Events:
1920: The 19th Amendment is ratified, allowing U.S. citizens of all sexes to vote.
1926: Famed magician Harry Houdini dies from peritonitis and a ruptured appendix.
1927: Charles Lindbergh becomes the first person to complete a trans-Atlantic flight.
1927: The first motion picture with audio, The Jazz Singer is released.
1929: The stock market crashes, leaving many unemployed and ending the Roaring Twenties, a decade of American prosperity.
1930: Mickey Mouse is created.
1936: The Hoover Dam is finished.
1936: Jesse Owens obtains four gold medals from the 1936 Summary Olympics, defying Adolf Hitler's belief in Aryan superiority.
1937: Amelia Earhart disappears flying over the Bermuda Triangle in an attempt to circumnavigate the world.
1938: Superman is created, beginning the trend of superheroes.
1939: Gone with the Wind is released, becoming the top grossing movie of all time, adjusted for inflation.
1939: The Manhattan Project begins in an attempt to develop the world's first nuclear weapons, with support from the UK and Canada.
