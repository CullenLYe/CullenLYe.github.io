1940-1959

Presidents During This Period:
1932-1945: Franklin D. Roosevelt
1945-1952: Harry S. Truman
1952-1960: Dwight D. Eisenhower


Notable Events:
1941: Mount Rushmore is finished in South Dakota, depicting the faces of George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt, and Abraham Lincoln.
1941: The Japanese launch a surprise attack on Pearl Harbor, an American naval base in Hawaii, prompting the United States' entry into World War 2.
1943: The cultural icon Rosie the Riveter emerges, encouraging women to participate in the war effort.
1944: American troops invade Normandy, France in Operation Overlord, establishing Allied presence in Nazi-dominated France.
1945: The United States drops two atomic bombs on Japan, effectively ending the global war.
1947: Charles Yeager, a United States Air Force officer, becomes the first person to break the sound barrier in flight.
1947: The Cold War begins with rising tensions between the United States and the Soviet Union.
1948: The Marshall Plan is passed, providing aid for Western Europe to rebuild, while also discouraging communism.
1948: In response to the Berlin Blockade, western powers instituted an airlift to provide food to citizens of West Berlin.
1950: America enters the Korean War.
1954: The Korean War ends.
1953: The spiral structure of DNA is discovered.
1955: Rosa Parks refuses to give up her seat on a bus in Montgomery, Alabama.
