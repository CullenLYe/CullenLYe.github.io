1960-1979

Presidents During This Period:
1952-1960: Dwight D. Eisenhower
1960-1963: John F. Kennedy
1963-1968: Lyndon B. Johnson
1968-1974: Richard Nixon
1974-1976: Gerald Ford
1976-1980: Jimmy Carter


Notable Events:
1964: The Beatles make their debut.
1965: The United States enter Vietnam to aid South Vietnam.
1968: Martin Luther King Jr., a Baptist civil rights activist, is assassinated.
1969: Neil Armstrong, an America, becomes the first man to step foot on the moon.
1970: Members of the Ohio National Guard open fire on Vietnam War protestors at Kent State University, killing four students.
1973: The Supreme Court case, Roe v. Wade, legalized abortion.
1973: Arab nations place an embargo on oil exports to the United States due to our support of Israel in the Yom Kippur War, leading to an oil crisis.
1974: Richard Nixon becomes the first U.S. President to resign from office, following his Watergate Scandal.
1975: The United States fully withdraws from Vietnam.
